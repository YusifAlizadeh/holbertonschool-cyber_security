# 🔍 Digital Forensics & Investigation Methods

Welcome to the **Forensics Methods** directory! This folder documents my training in digital investigations, focusing on the identification, preservation, and analysis of digital evidence.

## 📌 Overview

Digital forensics is the intersection of computer science and investigative methodology. This repository reflects my hands-on work with Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and the theoretical foundations required for modern forensic analysts.

## 🗂️ Deep Dive into Topics

### 1. 🌍 Geolocation & OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)
Practical "Geolocation Missions" where the objective is to find a specific point on Earth using only a single image or a vague clue:
* **Landmark Identification:** Using architectural styles, vegetation, and topography to narrow down regions.
* **Google Maps/Earth Analysis:** Utilizing satellite imagery, 3D viewing, and Street View to confirm coordinates.
* **Metadata & EXIF Data:** Understanding how hidden data in digital files can reveal GPS coordinates, camera models, and timestamps.
* **Visual Triangulation:** Calculating positions based on the shadows (sun position), street signs, and utility pole designs.

### 2. 📖 Core Forensics Terminology
Mastery of professional language used in the field:
* **Chain of Custody:** The chronological documentation showing the seizure, custody, control, and analysis of physical or electronic evidence.
* **Anti-Forensics:** Techniques used to hide or destroy evidence (e.g., steganography, file wiping, or encryption).
* **Live vs. Dead Acquisition:** Learning when to pull data from a running system (RAM) versus a powered-off device.
* **Write Blockers:** The importance of hardware/software tools that prevent any accidental modification of the source media during imaging.
* **Hashing (MD5/SHA):** Ensuring evidence integrity by creating a unique digital fingerprint of the data.

### 3. 🛡️ Investigative Mindset
* **The Locard's Exchange Principle:** "Every contact leaves a trace." Applying this fundamental rule of forensics to the digital world.
* **Evidence Preservation:** Best practices for creating bit-for-bit copies (Forensic Images) of storage media.

## 🛠️ Tools & Methodology

* **Google Maps Platform:** For advanced spatial reconnaissance.
* **OSINT Frameworks:** Applying structured search techniques to find public records and digital footprints.
* **Search Operators:** Using advanced Google Dorks and specialized search engines to extract hidden info.

## 📝 Learning Objectives

* Develop the "detective's eye" for spotting subtle clues in visual data.
* Understand the legal and procedural standards required to make digital evidence admissible in a court of law.
* Transition from a casual user to a professional investigator who knows where and how to look.
